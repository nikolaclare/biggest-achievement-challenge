Conquering the Great Victorian Rail Trail

A Solo Journey of Gravel Cycling and Bikepacking

The Journey Begins

Cycling has always been a passion, but last year I decided to push myself further. The Great Victorian Rail Trail was my longest ride, covering over 240 km across four days...

Riding along the Great Victorian Rail Trail was not just about cycling; it was an immersive experience filled with natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and community spirit. Each section offered unique sights and challenges that made every day memorable.

The Journey: Day-By-Day

Day 1: Tallarook to Molesworth - 55km

The adventure began in Tallarook, a charming town steeped in history and surrounded by scenic landscapes. With excitement and anticipation, I set off on the first leg of my solo cycling journey, covering approximately 55 kilometers through the stunning Trawool Valley.

The ride was relatively smooth, thanks to the mostly flat terrain, which provided a comfortable introduction to the trail. As I pedaled, the picturesque countryside unfolded before me—rolling hills, lush greenery, and the serene Goulburn River winding alongside much of the path. The peaceful sounds of nature and the sight of towering trees made the experience feel immersive and tranquil.

One of the highlights of the day was passing through the Cheviot Tunnel, the longest rail trail tunnel in Victoria. This historic structure, hidden among dense forest, was both an awe-inspiring sight and a welcome break from the midday sun.

After a full day of riding, I arrived in Molesworth, a quiet and almost deserted stopover. With nothing open and no one in sight, I was grateful for the simple yet life-saving provision left at the accommodation—bread and Vegemite. It was a quirky end to the day, but it added to the adventure, making the first night all the more memorable.

Day 2: Molesworth to Mansfield - 66km

A long stretch with some challenging climbs and beautiful views along the way...

On the second day, I set off from Molesworth towards Mansfield, covering about 66 kilometers. This segment offered a mix of gentle inclines and declines, providing a bit more challenge than the previous day. The landscape transitioned from lush farmland to more rugged terrain as I approached Mansfield.

Along the way, I encountered various art installations that celebrated local culture and history, enhancing the ride's aesthetic appeal. The trail also featured several water crossings, allowing for refreshing breaks. Upon reaching Mansfield, I book myself in for a well-earned massage and enjoyed a hearty meal at the local pub.

Day 3: Mansfield to Yea - 83km

This was the toughest day, but also the most rewarding, reaching the longest distance...

The ride from Mansfield back to Yea was approximately 83 kilometers long. This route was particularly memorable due to its breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. This day was marked by an exhilarating downhill stretch that made all the effort worthwhile.

Encountered an unexpected detour due to a farmer blocking the trail to Alexandra. As dusk was approaching had to back track and head to Yea for the night which added kilometres to an already long ride.

Day 4: Yea to Tallarook - 39km

The final stretch...

he final leg of my journey took me back to Tallarook from Yea, covering around 38 kilometers once more. This section felt familiar yet fresh as I appreciated the landscape anew. The ride was mostly flat but included some gentle gradients that kept things interesting.

As I retraced my path, I made sure to stop at some favorite spots along the way, including scenic overlooks and local landmarks that had caught my eye previously. Completing this loop felt rewarding, bringing my adventure full circle back to where it all began.

About this website »
